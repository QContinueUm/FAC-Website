why i'm applying

why i'm applying

i heard about founders and coders in 2016, around the time I moved
to the UK. i'd been interested in learning to code since 2004, something
i was introduced to while interning for a website called Boston.com. i was
a content producer and i worked closely with the in-house dev team. at that
time, it wasn't meant to be. i moved on to administrative work, and eventually
social justice work, but all the while the interest remained and often grew to
fever pitch, particularly when i would be looking for a tech solution that didn't
yet exist.

in non-profit work you are called to wear many hats with limited resources. this
always put me into the position of problem-solver and i always looked to tech solutions
to support the work i, and my team, did. bootcamps always intrigued me, but the cost was
always prohibitively expensive. solo learning was something i dabbled with, but without
peers and structure to keep me to task and to collaborate with, it was something that i
had difficulty finding the time and committment for.

upon learning about the peer-led model that founders and coders uses, my interest was
piqued. the program's core values align with my own: the value of community, comfort with
uncertainty, and a focus on helping others/group collaboration. the organization's commitment
to inclusivity, diversity, and good allyship all deeply resonate with other kinds of work i've
been engaged in, and work that i want to continue to engage in.

i am curious, open, eager to collaborate with and learn from others. a coding skillset would
open a lot of doors for me, as someone who has continuously hit the glass ceiling in all my
endeavors. i want to be part of a dynamic environment that's dedicated to group progress over
the individual; founders and coders seems to me a vision of how the tech industry could be,
a vision of the kind of tech industry i want to be part of.
