who i am

right. so, hello! i'm nico, which you know already.
i'm an immigrant here, from America. (she/they, by the way.)
my mum was an immigrant to America, from Colombia.
and my partner is an immigrant to the UK, from Turkey.

i guess you could say that immigration runs in my family
and is a core part of my identity and values (immigration rights!)

i'm latinx, queer, neurodivergent, and a lifelong learner.
i've had the privilege of a long career working with
non-profits engaged in social justice work and in independent
film (but not often enough simultaneously).

i come from privilege and am committed to the dismantling of systemic racism.
i believe antiracism is not something you are but something you do.

i value the communal experience and seek out communities centered
around inclusivity and diversity. i believe humans are at their best
when they come together and collaboratively approach problems.

every role i've had has called me to be a consummate problem-solver.
tech can be prohibitiely inaccessible to non-profit organizations;
i'm drawn to and motivated by the idea of bridging that gap.


this portrait of me by my 4 year old godson, Bora, best represents me.

i'd like to give special thanks to my partner who's taken on the
the bulk of our shared duties while i've prepared this application,
and to the friend housing us while we're "between homes."

what i want to do


as mentioned in my intro section, i have worked with non-profits
for the entirety of my working life. i worked as a program associate
for a fund that supports grassroots organizations in India centered around
union organizing, women's rights, trans rights, indigenous rights, and
rights groups centered around caste-based discrimination. i have also
worked in independent film for nearly as long.

i'd love to support work in these areas, whether by working on tech
that makes the lives of on-the-ground organizers easier, or improves
access to resources (be it data sharing, statistics, or training others).

i'm interested in contributing to Code for Social Good, Catch a Fire,
and/or similar organizations. Organizations centered around prison reform,
activism, immigration rights, and teaching girls to code all resonate too.
as someone invested in visual storytelling, the possibilities of putting
coding skills to work in game development (for example We Are Chicago)
feel further afield but of no lesser interest too.
